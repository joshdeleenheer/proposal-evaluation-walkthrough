Industrial Proposal Evaluation Framework

Evaluating a Proposal on Your Territory

A step-by-step walkthrough to help your Nation weigh an industrial proposal, see where it stands against best practice, and decide what to ask for.

When a company proposes a project — a mine, a pipeline, an energy facility — on your territory, the decisions come fast and the stakes are long. This tool walks you through the questions that matter most, in plain language. At each step you choose what fits your situation, and the tool shows you the strongest option alongside alternatives that may suit your circumstances better.

One proposal, two separate relationships

The most important thing to understand before you start: a proposal involves two tracks that run in parallel, and one cannot stand in for the other.

Track 1 · The Crown
Government's duties to you

Canada and BC owe your Nation duties that exist whether or not you ever sign a deal with the company: to consult and seek your consent, to share government revenue, to assess the build-up of all development, and to make sure the site is cleaned up. Signing a company deal does not let the government off the hook for any of this.

Track 2 · The Company
Your deal with the proponent (the IBA)

A private contract between your Nation and the company over jobs, contracts, money, equity, and project-specific protections. It is valuable — but it is not consent, it does not replace government revenue sharing, and it cannot fix cumulative effects or guarantee clean-up on its own.

This walkthrough is built in matching sections so you can check both tracks — and so new sections can be added as the research grows.

At the end you get a shareable summary — a plain-language snapshot of where the proposal stands across both tracks and a clear list of what to ask for, and of whom. You can print it or save it to bring into the room.
